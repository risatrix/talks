How to build a terrible CMS

Have no content strategy.

Make up a taxonomy as you go.

Say yes to every feature request.

Pay no attention to workflow.

Don't think of internal staff as users.

Build everything yourself.

Make your CMS a Swiss army knife.

Specs (The Hard Way)

A story!

(Headline, body, summary, slug)

By an author (byline)

No, actually, several authors

And each of their names need to link to a bio

So of course we need an author page

and the story needs a category. And a tag.

And there should probably be category and tag pages.

And the story needs lead art (captions? credits? is it content too?)

And related stories!

And it needs to be readable via rss

And it needs to be publishable in the future

And it needs a draft state for editing

And a "trashed" state, probably.

Logins and passwords and permissions, oh my!

And how are we going to organize the front page???

Oh — and ADS.

AND WHAT ABOUT COOL JAVASCRIPT STUFF???

And we need to be able to change the nav bar.

And integrate with Mailchimp/Evenbrite/CRM

Etc.

Most Stuff Can Happen OUTSIDE the CMS

Why? Because most CMS writing interfaces suck.

So does managing in-CMS JavaScript!

Alternatives:

Write in Wjordpress or Google Docs, then import into CMS.

Data entry in Google spreadsheets + Tabletop.js = cool, embeddable CYOA games and quizzes

Make quizzes here - end embed them.

How to Build a non-terrible CMS

Define content before building, with stakeholders.

Don't assume people will write in the CMS.

Make user stories. Use wireframes.

Iterate thoughtfully. Write tests.

Use what other people have built.

Define must-haves versus nice-to-haves.

Build quickly and break things -- on your own time.
